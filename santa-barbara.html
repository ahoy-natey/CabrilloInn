Welcome to Santa Barbara

Nestled between the Pacific Ocean and the Santa Ynez Mountains, Santa Barbara is a coastal paradise known for its Mediterranean climate, Spanish colonial architecture, and vibrant culture. From pristine beaches to world-class wineries, there's something for every traveler to discover.

Must-See Attractions

Mission Santa Barbara

Known as the "Queen of the Missions," this historic landmark showcases stunning Spanish colonial architecture and beautiful gardens. Founded in 1786, it's one of the most photographed missions in California.

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon for the best lighting and fewer crowds.

Stearns Wharf

California's oldest working wharf offers stunning ocean views, fresh seafood restaurants, and unique shops. Take a sunset stroll or enjoy a meal with panoramic Pacific views.

Don't Miss: The sea lions that often sunbathe on the wharf's pilings.

Santa Barbara County Courthouse

This architectural masterpiece features Spanish-Moorish design, beautiful murals, and a clock tower with panoramic city views. Free guided tours available.

Pro Tip: Visit the clock tower for the best views of the city and ocean.

Outdoor Activities

Beach Activities

  • East Beach: Perfect for swimming, volleyball, and picnics
  • Butterfly Beach: Ideal for sunset watching and romantic walks
  • Leadbetter Beach: Great for surfing and paddleboarding
  • Arroyo Burro Beach: Dog-friendly beach with hiking trails

Hiking & Nature

  • Inspiration Point: Moderate 3.5-mile hike with city and ocean views
  • Seven Falls: Beautiful waterfall hike in the Santa Ynez Mountains
  • Douglas Family Preserve: Coastal bluff trails with ocean vistas
  • Botanic Garden: Native California plants and peaceful walking paths

Wine Country

  • Santa Barbara Wine Country: Over 200 wineries in the region
  • Urban Wine Trail: Tasting rooms in downtown Santa Barbara
  • Santa Ynez Valley: Scenic drives through rolling vineyards
  • Wine Tours: Guided tours available from downtown

Local Dining

Santa Barbara's culinary scene reflects its coastal location and agricultural heritage. From fresh seafood to farm-to-table restaurants, you'll find exceptional dining options throughout the city.

Seafood & Coastal Cuisine

Fresh catches from the Pacific, including local halibut, sea bass, and Santa Barbara spot prawns. Don't miss the famous Santa Barbara sea urchin (uni).

Farm-to-Table

Restaurants featuring produce from the Santa Barbara County's rich agricultural region, including avocados, strawberries, and artichokes.

Wine & Dine

Many restaurants offer extensive wine lists featuring local Santa Barbara County wines, perfect for pairing with your meal.

Weather & Best Time to Visit

Mediterranean Climate

Santa Barbara enjoys a mild Mediterranean climate with warm, dry summers and cool, wet winters. The average temperature ranges from 60°F in winter to 75°F in summer.

Spring (March-May)

Wildflowers bloom, gardens burst with color, and mild weather is perfect for hiking and exploring outdoors.

Summer (June-August)

Sunny days, lively beaches, and outdoor festivals—enjoy Santa Barbara at its most vibrant with endless coastal fun.

Fall (September-November)

Harvest season brings golden light, wine tasting, and crisp evenings—ideal for scenic drives and local flavors.

Winter (December-February)

Peaceful gardens, world-class museums, and whale watching—discover Santa Barbara’s charm with fewer crowds.

Getting Around Santa Barbara

Downtown & Beach Areas

Most attractions in downtown Santa Barbara and along the waterfront are easily walkable. The city is pedestrian-friendly with wide sidewalks and crosswalks.

Public Transportation

Santa Barbara MTD buses provide convenient service throughout the city and to nearby areas. The Electric Shuttle offers free rides in the downtown area. Check online for more details

Biking

Santa Barbara is a bike-friendly city with dedicated bike lanes and the scenic Cabrillo Boulevard bike path along the waterfront.

Car & Parking

While a car isn't necessary for downtown exploration, it's useful for visiting wineries, hiking trails, and nearby attractions. Street parking and public lots are available throughout the city.
